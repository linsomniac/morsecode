Settings

How fast each dit and dah is sent. At W WPM, one dit = 1200/W ms (PARIS standard). Higher WPM means shorter, faster elements. 18
Set lower than character speed to keep dits and dahs fast while stretching the gap between prompts. Gives you more thinking time without slowing the sound. Capped at character WPM. 13
Sidetone pitch in Hertz. 600 Hz is a common amateur-radio default — pick whatever's comfortable for long sessions. 600
Uses the browser's speech synthesis to say the letter aloud right before the Morse plays. Useful when first introducing a new character.
Whether to play the Morse for you before you key it. Adaptive fades it out per character once your recent accuracy passes 85%. Always keeps it on; never makes you recall the Morse from the letter alone.
Whether to show the Morse symbols (· −) under the letter. Same modes as audio aid — adaptive fades per character once you're consistently right.
When on: holding a paddle generates dits or dahs at the trainer's WPM, and squeezing both alternates dit-dah (Mode A). When off: each keypress sends exactly one element — closer to a straight key. OS auto-repeat is suppressed either way.
